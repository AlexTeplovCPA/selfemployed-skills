## 01. Prior Year Documents

- [ ] **2024 Notice of Assessment (NOA)**: Contains your RRSP deduction limit, loss carry-forwards, and instalment threshold; access via CRA My Account if you cannot locate the original.
- [ ] **Copy of 2024 T1 return** (new clients only): Used to confirm carry-forward amounts and prior year elections; request from your prior preparer or export from tax software.
- [ ] **Prior-year CCA schedule showing UCC balances by class** (new clients only): Required to calculate 2025 CCA correctly; ask your prior preparer specifically for the CCA continuity schedule if it is not in the T1 PDF.
- [ ] **2025 tax instalment payment confirmation**: Total amount paid and date of each payment; confirm via CRA My Account under Tax instalments.
- [ ] **CRA My Account and My Business Account access confirmed**: Verify active access before submitting your package; both accounts allow you to confirm balances, filing history, and correspondence.
- [ ] **Any CRA correspondence or reassessments from 2024 or 2025**: Provide all letters without filtering; reassessments change RRSP room and loss carry-forwards.

## 02. Personal Information

Provide updates only for items that changed in 2025.

- [ ] **Province of residence on December 31, 2025**: Determines which provincial tax rates apply to your full return; if you moved provinces, provide both provinces and the date of the move.
- [ ] **Marital status as of December 31, 2025**: Affects spousal credits, GST/HST credit, and RRSP spousal contributions; if status changed, provide the date and nature of the change.
- [ ] **Spouse or common-law partner: name, SIN, and 2025 net income**: Required for spousal credit and benefit calculations.
- [ ] **Dependant information**: Name, date of birth, and SIN for each dependant; include childcare expenses paid and provider SIN or business number.
- [ ] **US citizenship or green card status**: US citizens have annual IRS filing obligations regardless of where they live; flag at intake so cross-border considerations can be addressed.

---

## 03. Employment Income

Complete this section only if you received T4 employment income in 2025 in addition to contract income.

- [ ] **T4 slips from all employers**: Include any T4 from a salaried position held before transitioning to contract; the combination of T4 and T4A income requires coordination on CPP contributions and tax withholdings.
- [ ] **Stock options, RSUs, or employer equity** (if applicable): Provide grant date, exercise or vesting date, exercise price, fair market value at exercise or vesting, and number of units; tax treatment differs significantly between CCPC options and public company RSUs.

---

## 04. Self-Employment Revenue

Report all revenue gross, before deducting expenses, platform fees, or any other amounts.

- [ ] **T4A slips from all clients and platforms**: Collect all T4As and confirm the total matches your invoicing records; CRA cross-references T4As against T2125 reported revenue.
- [ ] **Full invoice list for 2025 or accounting revenue report**: A complete record of all invoices issued, including client, date, amount, currency, and paid or unpaid status; if books are current in Xero or QuickBooks, an income report for January 1 to December 31 is sufficient.
- [ ] **Client contracts, SOWs, and engagement letters**: Signed copies for all engagements active in 2025; confirm the nature of services, billing terms, and basis for GST/HST treatment on each invoice.
- [ ] **Work-in-progress: revenue earned but not yet invoiced at December 31** (if applicable): Fixed-fee engagements where work was performed in 2025 but invoicing is scheduled for 2026 may still be 2025 income under accrual accounting; flag open engagements at year-end.
- [ ] **Platform payout summaries and annual information reports**: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, Deel, and similar platforms; include earnings summaries, fee breakdowns, and any platform-issued tax information statement.
- [ ] **Payment processor reports and fee summaries**: Stripe, PayPal, Wise, Revolut Business; download the annual summary and transaction-level CSV export; FX conversion fees are deductible but frequently missed.
- [ ] **Business bank statements for all 12 months of 2025**: Used to reconcile deposits against invoices and identify missing revenue; maintain a dedicated business account separate from personal.
- [ ] **US client income: 1099, 1042-S, or direct payment records**: Provide total USD amounts, payment dates, and exchange rate or CAD equivalent; if US withholding tax was deducted, a foreign tax credit may apply.
- [ ] **Reimbursed expenses from clients** (if any): Note amounts and corresponding expenses; confirm reimbursements are captured as income and underlying expenses are claimed consistently to avoid double-counting.
- [ ] **Crypto-asset payment records** (if applicable): Date, amount, CAD fair market value at receipt, and wallet or transaction exports; crypto received for services is business income at fair market value on the date received.
- [ ] **Other income without slips**: Cash payments, e-transfers, referral fees, or income from platforms that do not issue Canadian tax slips; all self-employment revenue is reportable regardless of payment method.

## 05. GST/HST Records

- [ ] **GST/HST registration number and filing frequency**: Your 9-digit Business Number, registration date, and whether you file monthly, quarterly, or annually.
- [ ] **GST/HST returns filed for 2025 and amounts remitted**: Copies of all returns filed for periods within or overlapping 2025; T2125 gross revenue and GST/HST taxable supplies must be consistent.
- [ ] **Total GST/HST collected and total ITCs claimed**: ITC claims must be supported by receipts showing HST paid and the supplier's registration number for amounts over $30.
- [ ] **Quick Method election or confirmation** (if applicable): Confirm the election is in place and provide 2025 gross revenue including HST collected; the election cannot be made retroactively for 2025.
- [ ] **Client location list for all 2025 clients**: Legal entity name and billing location (province or country) for every client invoiced; required for place-of-supply review and to confirm GST/HST treatment on each invoice.
- [ ] **Sample invoices showing how tax was applied**: One example for a Canadian client with GST/HST charged, one for a non-resident client if applicable, and one platform payout example; zero-rating requires documentation beyond a non-Canadian billing address.
- [ ] **Threshold check if not yet registered**: Provide total gross revenue by month for 2024 and 2025 to confirm whether and when the $30,000 small-supplier threshold was exceeded.

## 06. Shared Expenses (Allocation Required)

Each item below requires a documented business-use percentage. The allocation must be defensible; it does not need to be perfect.

- [ ] **Home workspace: total home square footage and workspace square footage**: Used to calculate the home office percentage applied to rent or mortgage interest, utilities, insurance, and property tax.
- [ ] **Rent receipts or mortgage interest details**: Renters: annual rent total. Homeowners: year-end mortgage interest summary from your lender (not an estimate from monthly payments).
- [ ] **Utility bills: electricity, heating, and water annual totals**: Annual totals are sufficient for intake; retain underlying statements for CRA review.
- [ ] **Internet: annual total and business-use percentage**: Allocation required; a household connection shared with family or used for personal streaming cannot be claimed at 100%.
- [ ] **Mobile phone: annual total and business-use percentage**: A dedicated business line can be claimed in full; a shared personal plan requires a percentage allocation.
- [ ] **Home insurance annual premium**: Allocation required based on workspace square footage percentage.
- [ ] **Property tax bill** (homeowners only): Allocation required based on workspace square footage percentage.
- [ ] **Co-working space receipts** (if applicable): Generally deductible in full; if claiming both home office and co-working space for overlapping periods, flag for review.
- [ ] **Large home-office equipment purchases over $500**: Standing desk, ergonomic chair, docking station, networking gear; may be capital assets requiring CCA treatment rather than a current expense deduction.
- [ ] **Business-use percentage notes for all mixed-use assets**: For any asset used for both personal and business purposes, provide the estimated percentage and a short rationale.
- [ ] **Mileage log for business vehicle use** (if applicable): Date, destination, business purpose, and kilometres for each trip; most IT contractors work remotely and will have no vehicle claim.

---

## 07. Direct Business Expenses

These expenses do not require a percentage allocation if used entirely or primarily for business.

- [ ] **Hardware and computer equipment**: Laptop, monitors, server, external drives, headset, webcam, test devices; hardware is generally a capital asset claimed through CCA, not a current expense deduction; the half-year rule applies in the year of acquisition.
- [ ] **Developer tools and coding subscriptions**: GitHub, JetBrains, Docker Desktop, Postman, CI/CD tooling; flag any multi-year prepaid contracts separately as only the 2025 portion is deductible.
- [ ] **Cloud infrastructure and hosting**: AWS, Azure, GCP, DigitalOcean, Vercel, Cloudflare, managed databases; download annual billing summaries and transaction-level exports from each provider.
- [ ] **AI and API usage costs**: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google AI, Hugging Face, vector database and LLM tooling; retain records distinguishing client work from personal experimentation as only the business-use portion is deductible.
- [ ] **SR&ED flag** (if applicable): If you conducted experimental development in 2025 (novel algorithms, custom model training, new AI architectures), flag at intake; SR&ED may provide an investment tax credit and requires dedicated review before filing.
- [ ] **General business SaaS tools**: Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Adobe Creative Cloud, project management, CRM, accounting software; flag any shared family plans that require allocation.
- [ ] **Security and compliance tooling**: Password manager, VPN, endpoint protection, backup services, monitoring tools; check for auto-renewals in the January-to-March window that relate to 2025 coverage periods.
- [ ] **Domains, hosting, SSL certificates, and business email**: Registration and renewal fees; pull billing history from your registrar to confirm all 2025 charges are captured.
- [ ] **Business telecom and collaboration tools**: Zoom, Slack, Teams, VOIP, and conferencing tools used for client and business communication.
- [ ] **Software or licences purchased for specific client delivery**: Items required for a specific engagement, not general business tools; confirm whether client reimbursed these costs and that reimbursements are captured as income.
- [ ] **Professional liability, E&O, and cyber liability insurance**: Policy invoices and annual premium totals; E&O insurance is fully deductible as a direct business expense.
- [ ] **Accounting and legal fees**: Teplov CPA fees for bookkeeping and return preparation; legal fees for reviewing or drafting client contracts; only the business-related portions are deductible.
- [ ] **Business banking fees, transfer fees, and FX conversion fees**: Pull from payment processor transaction exports, not bank statements alone; FX fees are deductible but frequently missed.
- [ ] **Platform fees and marketplace commissions**: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal commissions; these appear in payout summaries rather than as separate invoices and must be identified separately from gross revenue.
- [ ] **Advertising, marketing, and lead generation**: Paid ads, LinkedIn Premium used for business development, portfolio website promotion, and lead-generation tools.
- [ ] **Business travel: flights, hotels, and ground transportation**: Separate from vehicle expenses in Section 06; travel to client sites, conferences, or documented business meetings.
- [ ] **Meals and entertainment** (business-related only): Include date, amount, venue, attendees, and business purpose for each receipt; only 50% of eligible amounts are deductible.
- [ ] **Professional development: courses, certifications, conferences**: Directly related to current IT contracting work; Canada Training Credit may also apply but cannot be claimed on the same costs as a T2125 business expense.
- [ ] **Subcontractor payments** (if applicable): Invoices, proof of payment, name and SIN or business number of each subcontractor; T4A reporting obligations may apply for payments over $500 in a calendar year.
- [ ] **PHSP premiums** (if applicable): Health and dental premiums through an eligible plan for yourself and household members; cannot be double-claimed as a personal medical expense credit on the T1.

## 08. Investment Income

- [ ] **T5 slips: interest and dividend income**: From all financial institutions; interest in a non-registered account is fully taxable; TFSA interest is not reportable.
- [ ] **T3 slips: trust and mutual fund income**: T3s are typically issued in March and sometimes delayed; do not file without confirming all T3s are in hand.
- [ ] **T5008: securities transactions**: Shows proceeds only; also provide the adjusted cost base and transaction costs for each disposition to calculate capital gain or loss.
- [ ] **Personal cryptocurrency transactions** (if applicable): Complete transaction history for crypto bought, sold, or traded for personal (non-business) purposes; each disposition is a separate taxable event reported on Schedule 3.
- [ ] **Foreign bank and investment accounts**: Confirm year-end balance and whether total cost of all specified foreign property exceeded $100,000 CAD at any point in 2025; T1135 penalties start at $25 per day.

---

## 09. Year-End Position

IT contractors reporting on Form T2125 generally use accrual accounting. These items are invisible from your bank account alone.

- [ ] **Year-end accounts receivable: invoices issued but unpaid at December 31**: List all outstanding invoices with client, date, number, and amount; invoices issued in 2025 are 2025 income even if payment arrives in January 2026.
- [ ] **Year-end accounts payable: expenses incurred but unpaid at December 31**: Business expenses owed at year-end are deductible in 2025 regardless of when paid; include subcontractor invoices, software renewals, and accounting or legal invoices outstanding at year-end.
- [ ] **Sale or disposal of business assets** (if applicable): Date, proceeds, original purchase date, and original cost for any equipment previously on a CCA schedule; may trigger recapture or terminal loss.
- [ ] **Bad debts** (if applicable): Invoice previously included in income that is genuinely uncollectible; provide original invoice details, year included in income, and documentation of collection attempts.

---

## 10. Registered Plans

- [ ] **RRSP contribution receipts: January 1, 2025 to March 2, 2026**: Collect from all institutions; first-60-days contributions can be deducted on the 2025 return or carried forward.
- [ ] **RRSP deduction election**: Confirm whether to deduct contributions in 2025 or carry forward to a higher-income year; flag if you want deduction timing modelled as part of return preparation.
- [ ] **FHSA: contributions and withdrawals** (if applicable): Provide the T4FHSA slip; 2025 annual limit is $8,000 with a $40,000 lifetime limit.
- [ ] **Home Buyers Plan repayment** (if applicable): Required 2025 repayment amount shown on your 2024 NOA; shortfall is added to 2025 taxable income if minimum not made by March 2, 2026.

---

## 11. Deductions and Personal Tax Credits

- [ ] **Medical expenses**: Eligible expenses paid in any 12-month period ending in 2025; request annual statements from pharmacy and dentist rather than gathering individual receipts; costs already deducted as PHSP expenses on T2125 cannot also be claimed here.
- [ ] **Charitable donation receipts**: Official receipts for all donations to registered Canadian charities in 2025; include unused carry-forwards from prior years shown on your 2024 NOA.
- [ ] **Childcare expenses** (if applicable): Amount paid, provider name, and provider SIN or business number; generally claimed by the lower-income spouse.
- [ ] **Moving expenses** (if applicable): If you moved at least 40 kilometres closer to a new place of business; provide receipts for transportation, storage, travel, and temporary accommodation.
- [ ] **T2202 tuition certificate** (if applicable): From any Canadian post-secondary institution attended in 2025; unused credits carry forward indefinitely.
- [ ] **Ontario Trillium Benefit** (Ontario residents only): Total property tax paid or total rent paid in 2025; include address and landlord details if renting.
- [ ] **Digital news subscription receipts**: Eligible subscriptions to qualifying Canadian news organizations in 2025.
- [ ] **Ontario Fertility Treatment Tax Credit** (Ontario residents only, if applicable): Eligible fertility treatment or surrogacy expenses paid in 2025 up to $20,000.

## 12. Incorporation and Structuring Flags

No documents required for this section. Answer these questions at intake.

- [ ] **Worker classification risk**: If you work primarily for one client on an ongoing basis, on-site, performing duties similar to an employee: flag the nature of the arrangement before filing; CRA may challenge whether the arrangement constitutes self-employment.
- [ ] **Incorporation timing question**: If you are considering incorporating in 2026, flag before the 2025 return is prepared; the 2025 T1 position affects optimal incorporation timing and structure.
- [ ] **Personal Services Business risk** (if already incorporated): If your corporation provides services to a single client where you would otherwise be considered an employee, flag at intake; PSB status significantly limits deductible expenses.

## 13. Digital Records and Audit Protection

CRA audits can arrive two to four years after filing. Download and store these records now; many platforms limit access after 12 to 24 months.

- [ ] **Annual billing summaries from all cloud and SaaS providers**: AWS, Azure, GCP, GitHub, JetBrains, OpenAI, Anthropic, and any other paid platform; download PDF and CSV exports now as access windows are limited.
- [ ] **Platform payout and fee records**: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, Deel; download before account closure or inactivity restricts access.
- [ ] **Payment processor annual reports and transaction exports**: Stripe, PayPal, Wise, Revolut Business; transaction-level exports are significantly more useful than bank statements for reconciling FX fees and income timing.
- [ ] **Crypto transaction records exported and preserved** (if applicable): Full history from all wallets and exchanges including timestamps, amounts, and CAD fair market value; exchanges exit markets without notice.
- [ ] **Invoice records backed up outside your invoicing tool**: Export PDF copies of all 2025 invoices plus a full invoice list CSV; SaaS invoicing platforms exist only as long as your account does.
- [ ] **Contract and SOW copies stored with 2025 tax records**: Signed copies of all client contracts active in 2025; download from DocuSign or similar platforms as PDFs.
- [ ] **Business-use evidence for AI and API tools**: Project notes, commit history, client deliverables, or a written record noting which client engagement each usage period relates to; a provider usage export alone is not sufficient.
- [ ] **All 2025 records organized in a dedicated tax folder**: Subfolders by category: income, GST/HST, shared expenses, direct expenses, year-end position, prior year; store a local backup copy.
