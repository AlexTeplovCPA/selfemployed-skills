## 01. Prior Year Corporate Documents

- [ ] **Prior year T2 corporate return** (new clients only): Full filed copy including all schedules; used to confirm opening balances, carry-forwards, and prior year elections.
- [ ] **Prior year corporate Notice of Assessment**: Issued by CRA after the T2 was assessed; confirms the assessed tax position and any outstanding balances or adjustments.
- [ ] **Prior year financial statements** (new clients only): Balance sheet and income statement for the fiscal year ending in 2024; opening balances on the 2025 return flow from these.
- [ ] **Prior year CCA schedule showing UCC balances by class**: Required to calculate 2025 CCA correctly; ask your prior preparer specifically for the CCA continuity schedule if it is not in the T2 PDF.
- [ ] **Prior year RDTOH, GRIP, and LRIP balances** (if applicable): Refundable Dividend Tax on Hand, General Rate Income Pool, and Low Rate Income Pool balances affect dividend refund calculations and eligible dividend designations; confirm balances from the prior year T2 Schedule 3 and Schedule 53.
- [ ] **CRA My Business Account access confirmed**: Verify active access before submitting your package; My Business Account shows corporate account balances, filing history, and outstanding returns or correspondence.
- [ ] **Any CRA correspondence or reassessments on the corporate account**: Provide all letters without filtering; reassessments affect opening balances and carry-forward positions.

## 02. Corporate Revenue

Report all revenue gross, before deducting expenses or any other amounts.

- [ ] **Full invoice list for the 2025 fiscal year or accounting revenue report**: All invoices issued during the fiscal year: client, date, amount, currency, and paid or unpaid status; if books are current in Xero or QuickBooks, an income report for the full fiscal year is sufficient.
- [ ] **T4A slips issued to the corporation** (if any): Some clients issue T4A slips to corporations rather than individuals; collect all T4As and confirm the total matches invoicing records.
- [ ] **Client contracts and SOWs active during the fiscal year**: Signed copies confirm the nature of services, billing terms, and basis for GST/HST treatment; also establishes that the corporation was providing services under a genuine commercial arrangement.
- [ ] **Platform payout summaries** (if applicable): Upwork, Toptal, Deel, and similar platforms; include annual earnings summaries and fee breakdowns.
- [ ] **Payment processor reports and fee summaries**: Stripe, PayPal, Wise, Revolut Business; download annual summary and transaction-level CSV; FX conversion fees are deductible but frequently missed.
- [ ] **Corporate bank statements for all months in the fiscal year**: Used to reconcile all deposits against invoices and identify missing revenue.
- [ ] **US client income: 1099 or direct payment records** (if applicable): Total USD amounts, payment dates, and CAD equivalent; foreign income must be converted using a consistent exchange-rate method.
- [ ] **Work-in-progress at fiscal year-end** (if applicable): Revenue earned but not yet invoiced at year-end may be reportable under accrual accounting; flag open fixed-fee engagements at year-end.

## 03. Corporate Expenses

- [ ] **Hardware and computer equipment purchases**: Laptop, monitors, server, external drives, and other equipment; hardware is generally a capital asset claimed through CCA; provide purchase date, vendor, amount, and business-use percentage for any mixed-use item.
- [ ] **Developer tools and software subscriptions**: GitHub, JetBrains, Docker, CI/CD tooling, and similar; flag multi-year prepaid contracts separately.
- [ ] **Cloud infrastructure and hosting**: AWS, Azure, GCP, DigitalOcean, and similar; download annual billing summaries and transaction-level exports.
- [ ] **AI and API usage costs**: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google AI, and similar; retain records distinguishing client work from personal or experimental use.
- [ ] **General business SaaS tools**: Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, project management, CRM, accounting software.
- [ ] **Professional liability, E&O, and cyber liability insurance**: Policy invoices and annual premium totals; fully deductible as a direct business expense.
- [ ] **Accounting and legal fees**: CPA fees for bookkeeping and T2 preparation; legal fees for contracts; only the business-related portions are deductible.
- [ ] **Business banking and FX fees**: Pull from payment processor transaction exports; FX fees are deductible but frequently missed.
- [ ] **Platform fees and marketplace commissions**: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal commissions embedded in payout summaries; must be identified separately from gross revenue.
- [ ] **Home office expenses** (if the corporation reimburses the shareholder): If the corporation pays a home office allowance or reimbursement to the owner, document the basis and amount; the reimbursement is a corporate expense and income to the owner.
- [ ] **Subcontractor payments** (if applicable): Invoices, proof of payment, name and business number of each subcontractor; T4A reporting obligations may apply for payments over $500 in a calendar year.
- [ ] **SR&ED flag** (if applicable): If experimental development work was conducted in 2025 (novel algorithms, custom model training, new AI architectures), flag at intake; SR&ED may provide a refundable investment tax credit for Canadian-controlled private corporations and requires dedicated review before filing.
- [ ] **Meals, entertainment, and business travel**: Receipts with date, amount, attendees, and business purpose; meals and entertainment are 50% deductible.
- [ ] **Professional development**: Courses, certifications, and conference registrations directly related to the current contracting work.

## 04. Shareholder Accounts and Compensation

This section is critical for incorporated IT contractors. Errors in shareholder compensation and loan accounts are among the most common and costly issues on IT contractor T2 returns.

- [ ] **Salary paid to shareholder-employees in the fiscal year**: Total gross salary paid, payroll remittances made to CRA, and T4 slips issued; confirm payroll accounts are current and all remittances are up to date.
- [ ] **Dividends declared and paid in the fiscal year**: Amount, date declared, date paid, and whether designated as eligible or non-eligible; dividends must be supported by a directors' resolution in the minute book.
- [ ] **Shareholder loan account balance at fiscal year-end**: Running balance of all amounts loaned to or from the shareholder; a debit balance (corporation owes the shareholder) is generally straightforward; a credit balance (shareholder owes the corporation) triggers Section 15(2) inclusion risk if not repaid within the required timeframe.
- [ ] **Shareholder loan transactions during the fiscal year**: All advances from the corporation to the shareholder and all repayments made; include dates and amounts for each transaction.
- [ ] **Management fees paid to the shareholder or related parties** (if applicable): Amount, recipient, and documentation of services performed; management fees require a genuine commercial basis and supporting documentation.
- [ ] **Personal expenses paid by the corporation** (if any): Any personal expenses paid through the corporate account that were not reimbursed; these are shareholder benefits and must be reported as income on the T1.

## 05. GST/HST Records (Corporate)

- [ ] **Corporate GST/HST registration number and filing frequency**: 9-digit Business Number and program account number; confirm registration date and whether filing monthly, quarterly, or annually.
- [ ] **GST/HST returns filed for the fiscal year and amounts remitted**: Copies of all returns and confirmations; corporate T2 revenue and GST/HST taxable supplies must be consistent.
- [ ] **Total GST/HST collected and total ITCs claimed**: ITC claims must be supported by receipts showing HST paid and the supplier's registration number for amounts over $30.
- [ ] **Client location list**: Legal entity name and billing location for every client invoiced; required for place-of-supply review and to confirm GST/HST treatment on each invoice including any zero-rating applied to non-resident clients.
- [ ] **Quick Method election or confirmation** (if applicable): Confirm election is in place; cannot be made retroactively.
- [ ] **Threshold check if not yet registered**: Provide total gross revenue by month to confirm whether and when the $30,000 small-supplier threshold was exceeded.

## 06. Year-End Position

Incorporated IT contractors report on accrual accounting. These items are invisible from bank statements alone.

- [ ] **Year-end accounts receivable**: All invoices issued and outstanding at fiscal year-end; include client, invoice date, number, and amount; accrual-basis income includes invoiced amounts not yet received.
- [ ] **Year-end accounts payable**: Business expenses incurred before fiscal year-end but not yet paid; include subcontractor invoices, software renewals, and accounting or legal invoices outstanding at year-end.
- [ ] **Sale or disposal of business assets** (if applicable): Date, proceeds, original purchase date, and original cost for any asset previously on a CCA schedule; may trigger recapture or terminal loss.
- [ ] **Unpaid salary accruals at year-end** (if applicable): Salary declared but not yet paid at fiscal year-end; unpaid amounts are only deductible in the year paid if paid within 180 days of fiscal year-end; flag any declared but unpaid salary.
- [ ] **Bad debts** (if applicable): Invoice previously included in income that is genuinely uncollectible; provide original invoice details, year included in income, and documentation of collection attempts.

## 07. Corporate Records and Minute Book

- [ ] **Certificate of incorporation and corporate registration** (new clients only): Confirms the legal name, incorporation date, jurisdiction, and business number.
- [ ] **Share register showing current shareholders and share classes**: Required to confirm who owns the corporation and the basis for any dividends paid; provide the current share register or a shareholder list with share class and quantity for each holder.
- [ ] **Directors' resolutions for dividends declared in the fiscal year**: Each dividend payment must be supported by a signed directors' resolution stating the amount, date, and share class; resolutions should be in the minute book.
- [ ] **Fiscal year-end confirmation**: Confirm the corporation's fiscal year-end date; the T2 covers the corporation's fiscal year, which may not be December 31.
- [ ] **Any changes to share structure, directors, or officers in the fiscal year**: New shareholders, share transfers, director changes, or amendments to the articles; these affect the T2 and may trigger separate provincial filings.

## 08. Digital Records and Audit Protection

CRA audits can arrive two to four years after filing. Download and store these records now.

- [ ] **Annual billing summaries from all cloud and SaaS providers**: AWS, Azure, GCP, GitHub, JetBrains, OpenAI, Anthropic; download PDF and CSV exports now as access windows are often limited to 12 to 24 months.
- [ ] **Payment processor annual reports and transaction exports**: Stripe, PayPal, Wise; transaction-level exports are significantly more useful than bank statements for reconciling FX fees and income timing.
- [ ] **Invoice records backed up outside your invoicing tool**: Export PDF copies of all fiscal year invoices plus a full invoice list CSV; SaaS invoicing platforms exist only as long as your account does.
- [ ] **Contract and SOW copies stored with corporate tax records**: Signed copies of all client contracts active in the fiscal year; download from DocuSign or similar platforms as PDFs.
- [ ] **Payroll records and remittance confirmations** (if salary was paid): T4 slips issued, payroll summaries, and CRA remittance confirmations for the fiscal year.
- [ ] **Directors' resolutions and minute book entries for the fiscal year**: Signed copies of all resolutions passed during the fiscal year, including dividend declarations and any major corporate decisions.
- [ ] **All fiscal year corporate records organized in a dedicated folder**: Subfolders by category: revenue, expenses, shareholder accounts, GST/HST, year-end position, corporate records; store a local backup copy.
