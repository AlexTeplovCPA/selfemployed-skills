# T2125 Document Checklist

Use this checklist with `collecting-tax-documents`.

The purpose of this file is to help organize common records needed to support T2125 (Statement of Business or Professional Activities) for a Canadian IT contractor.

This checklist is for document collection only. It does not determine deductibility, GST/HST treatment, or final T2125 line placement.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

For each item below, mark one of:

- `received`
- `mentioned_not_provided`
- `missing`
- `not_applicable`
- `unclear`

Do not guess.

If the user is unsure whether a document exists, mark it as `unclear`.

---

## Core Business Income Support

### Invoice and revenue records

- full invoice summary for the year
- client-by-client income breakdown if available
- copies of major invoices if relevant

### Platform and payment summaries

- Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer, Toptal summaries if used
- Stripe, PayPal, Wise, Deel or similar payment summaries
- payout reports or transaction exports

### Slip-based income related to business

- T4A slips connected to contractor activity
- other slips tied to business income if mentioned

### Deposit and reconciliation support

- bank account statement used for business
- deposit summary or reconciliation to income totals
- bookkeeping export showing income entries if available

---

## Core Business Expense Support

### Expense records

- expense log or spreadsheet
- bookkeeping export showing expenses
- categorized transaction report if available

### Receipts and supporting documents

- receipts for major expenses
- invoices for recurring services
- proof of payment for significant items

---

## GST/HST Support (if applicable)

- GST/HST registration confirmation if registered
- GST/HST filing summaries for the year
- record of GST/HST collected
- record of input tax credits (ITCs) claimed or tracked

If the user is unsure about GST/HST status, mark items as `unclear`.

---

## Home Office Support (if applicable)

- workspace size or percentage estimate
- total home costs (rent, utilities, etc.)
- explanation of how the workspace is used for business

---

## Vehicle Support (if applicable)

- logbook or mileage record
- summary of business vs personal use
- fuel, insurance, and maintenance records

---

## Capital and Equipment Support

- receipts or invoices for equipment purchases
- details of items such as:
  - laptop
  - monitor
  - phone
  - office furniture
- approximate purchase dates

Do not assume treatment. Only collect support.

---

## Professional and Other Business Costs

- accounting or tax preparation invoices
- legal invoices if relevant
- subcontractor or freelancer payment records
- agreements or invoices for outside services

---

## Relevance Notes for This Repository

For most Canadian IT contractors, the highest-priority T2125 records are:

- invoice or income summary
- platform or payment summaries if used
- expense log or bookkeeping export
- receipts for major expenses
- GST/HST records if registered or possibly required

Other items should be collected only if the user’s situation suggests they are relevant.

---

## Output Guidance

When using this checklist inside a skill, group the result into:

### Confirmed T2125 documents
List all items marked `received`.

### Mentioned but not provided
List all items marked `mentioned_not_provided`.

### Missing T2125 documents
List only the missing items that materially affect the next workflow step.

### Not applicable
List items clearly outside the user’s situation only if doing so improves clarity.

---

## Common Red Flags During Collection

Flag but do not resolve:

- income mentioned but no invoice or summary provided
- platform income mentioned but no payout records
- expenses provided with no receipts or support
- GST/HST mentioned but no records provided
- home office claim mentioned with no details
- large equipment purchase with no documentation
- bank deposits not tied to any known income source

---

## Important Boundary

This checklist helps collect T2125-related records.

It does not:
- determine deductibility
- determine capital vs current treatment
- determine GST/HST obligations
- confirm completeness
- replace CPA review
